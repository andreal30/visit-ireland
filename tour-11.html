Hike & Explore: The Wild Wicklow Way

Conquer Rugged Beauty in Wicklow Mountains National Park

General Description

Lace up your boots and prepare for an exhilarating 5-day adventure through the untamed wilderness of Wicklow Mountains National Park. This immersive hiking experience challenges you with breathtaking trails, rewards you with panoramic vistas, and allows you to reconnect with nature's tranquility. Explore ancient ruins nestled amidst the mountains, discover hidden waterfalls, and experience the untamed beauty of the "Wicklow Way."

Itinerary

  1. Day 1 - Dublin Arrival & Transfer to Wicklow: Arrive in Dublin, Ireland's vibrant capital. After settling into your comfortable accommodation, enjoy some free time to explore the city or unwind after your journey. In the afternoon, a scenic transfer whisks you away to the charming gateway town of your Wicklow Mountains adventure.
  2. Day 2 - Glenmacmnassig Hike (moderate): Embark on your first hike, tackling the moderate Glenmacmnassig trail. Wind your way through lush valleys, past cascading waterfalls, and alongside the fast-flowing Glenmacnass River. Enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and soak in the fresh mountain air. Keep your eyes peeled for local wildlife like red deer and birds of prey soaring overhead. After a rewarding hike, settle into your comfortable accommodation in the heart of the National Park.
  3. Day 3 - Sally Gap & Lough Tay Hike (challenging): Gear up for the most challenging leg of your adventure - the Sally Gap and Lough Tay hike. This route ascends through dramatic mountain terrain, offering breathtaking vistas of the Wicklow landscape. Conquer the Sally Gap, a scenic mountain pass, and marvel at the glacial lake of Lough Tay, nicknamed the "Guinness Lake" for its distinctive dark color. This challenging yet rewarding hike will leave you breathless in every sense of the word. In the evening, enjoy a hearty meal and well-deserved rest at your cozy accommodation.
  4. Day 4 - Powerscourt Waterfall & Glendalough Monastic Site: Trade your hiking boots for comfortable shoes and embark on a cultural exploration. Visit the majestic Powerscourt Waterfall, the highest waterfall in Ireland, and witness its cascading waters surrounded by lush greenery. In the afternoon, delve into Irish history with a visit to the Glendalough Monastic Site. Explore the ruins of an early Christian settlement dating back to the 6th century, including the Round Tower, churches, and high crosses. Learn about the lives of early Irish monks and immerse yourself in the serene beauty of the valley.
  5. Day 5 - Wicklow to Dublin & Departure: Enjoy a scenic transfer back to Dublin, reflecting on the incredible experiences of your hiking adventure. Take some time to explore Dublin's vibrant streets or do some last-minute souvenir shopping before your departure. Bid farewell to Ireland with a heart full of memories and a newfound appreciation for the beauty and challenge of the Wicklow Mountains.
